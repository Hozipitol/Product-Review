A70

Honest review after 1 week of usage... Display (9/10): At 6.7 inch the display is quite big and it's bright in both indoors and outdoors... colours are rich, punchy and pops out of the screen with inky blacks make media consumption an awesome experience... Performance (9/10): The Snapdragon 675 coupled with the new and refreshing One UI at the top of android pie makes the user experience very smooth and fluid with zero stutter over the interface... gaming experience is smooth and can play pubg in high settings with zero lag or stutter... Battery (8/10): Battery life is great can easily get 2 days of usage with moderate use and 1 day of use with heavy usage... am a heavy user so I got the battery life as I desired around 20-30% of battery juice still left at the end of the day... with 25w bundled charger it can top up the battery to 100% in 80 minutes... Camera (8/10): Pictures taken on outdoors with the main sensor are really bright, punchy with lots of details even when zoomed in... HDR is great and when kicks in the results are great with good contrast ratio, natural colours and great details even in the shadows... the ultra wide sensor with 123 degrees field of view can really cramp in more in the screen and results are good and refreshing seen with new perspective... the depth sensor helps with good portrait images the subject and the background are well seperated producing natural bohkey effects... the 32 megapixel selfie camera is great producing lovely looking selfies with natural skin tone levels and bright picture... Video (8/10): The back main sensor can shoot videos at 4k and 1080p at 60 fps... the results are great with good HDR levels and no focus hunting but the results are shaky due to lack of any stabilization... Videos taken from ultra wide angle sensor is also good with acceptable detail levels... the video taken with the front camera is good and the sound captured is also good and audible... Overall (8/10): The A70 is a great phone with great crisp display, fantastic performance, great battery life and good set of cameras... the pricing is little on the higher side but considering you are paying the premium for the brand name Samsung and the after sales support it is a great option around 25-30k...

By: Saksham Shah
